Our Mission

I-neat bridges the gap between "knowing it's hot" and "knowing how to survive it" by turning raw weather data into life-saving daily habits. Because in a world where 40°C is becoming the "new normal," staying cool isn't just about comfort—it's about public health resilience.

The Problem

While the sun is a constant, our tolerance isn't. We're tackling the silent escalation of heat-related health crises in urban areas. As the heat index hits "Danger" levels more frequently, people—especially students and commuters—are "toughing it out" until they collapse.

Our Solution

I-neat isn't just a thermometer; it's a personal heat bodyguard. We provide predictive awareness, smart action plans, and community mapping to turn a lonely struggle into a shared effort to find the "coolest" spots in the city.

SDG 3: Good Health and Well-Being

I-neat supports health and well-being by helping users avoid heatstroke, dehydration, and heat exhaustion through alerts, reminders, and nearby cool zone guidance.

SDG 13: Climate Action

I-neat promotes climate awareness by helping people respond to extreme heat, adapt to rising temperatures, and make safer daily decisions during hot weather.

Why This Matters

Heatstroke has a high mortality rate, and dehydration kills productivity and mental clarity long before it kills the body. In a world where 40°C is becoming the "new normal," staying cool isn't just about comfort—it's about public health resilience.
