Why Outfit Matters

Dark clothing absorbs more heat from the sun, while light colors reflect it. The fabric and coverage also affect how your body regulates temperature.

Pro Tips

  • For heat: wear loose, light-colored clothing
  • For humidity: choose cotton or moisture-wicking fabrics
  • For strong sun: use a cap or umbrella
  • For rain: bring an umbrella/raincoat and wear non-slip shoes
  • For long outdoor trips: bring water and extra clothes

Heat Risk Levels Explained

Low Risk (Below 35°C)

Safe for outdoor activities. Stay hydrated and take breaks in shade.

Moderate Risk (35-40°C)

Limit prolonged outdoor exposure. Drink water every 15-20 minutes.

High Risk (40-45°C)

Avoid outdoor activities during peak hours. Seek air-conditioned spaces.

Extreme Risk (Above 45°C)

Heat emergency. Stay indoors with AC. Only go outside if absolutely necessary.
